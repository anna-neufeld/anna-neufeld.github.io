About

Hi! I am a third year PhD student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Washington. My current research is in selective inference, and I am advised by Daniela Witten .

Before coming to UW, I recieved undergraduate degrees in mathematics and computer science at Williams College . At Williams, I researched dynamics of infectious disease with Julie Blackwood as part of the SMALL REU and I wrote a senior thesis about regression trees for longitudinal data with Brianna Heggeseth.

I am passionate about statistics education and love teaching and working with undergraduates. I co-founded and currently serve on the organizing committee for the statistics Directed Reading Program at UW.

Current and Past Projects

Inference for regression trees.

Working with Daniela Witten and Lucy Gao on valid inference for regression trees. In particular, given a regression tree, how can one test for a true difference in means between two terminal nodes, or obtain a valid confidence interval for the true mean in a given terminal node? As the terminal nodes are chosen by looking at the data, classical inference is invalid.

Longitudinal Regression Trees

For my senior thesis at Williams College, I worked with Professor Brianna Heggeseth on using regression trees to cluster longitudinal data. We were particularly motivated by clustering Body Mass Index (BMI) trajectories by their shape as opposed to by their level. I created created the R package splinetree, which implements regression trees and random forests for longitudinal data using a modified version of a spline projection method first proposed by Yu and Lambert (1999). The method allows users to build and evaluate trees using either the shape only or the shape and level of a trajectory. The R package is available on CRAN .

Modeling the Dynamics of Zika Virus.

Worked with Julie Blackwood and Lauren Childs alongside two other undergraduates to quantify the contribution of sexual transmission in the spread of Zika virus and to study whether or not the asymmetric nature of sexual transmission could place females at higher risk than males. This work was part of the SMALL REU at Williams College in the summer of 2016 and was supported by a Clare Boothe Luce fellowship. The work resulted in a paper in Epidemics .
